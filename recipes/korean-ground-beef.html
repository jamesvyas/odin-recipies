Korean Gochujang Ground Beef

Description

A sweet and spicy dinner that's easy to whip up in limited time with just ground beef and pantry ingredients (you do keep gochujang and gochugaru on hand, right?). Any ground meat can be used.

You can alter the spice level by buying mild or hot gochujang, but the gochugaru is fairly non-negotiable, it provides a brightness to the dish. Regular dried chili flakes are an acceptable substitute for heat, but flavor will change.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef or other ground meat
  • 1/2 cup gochujang paste
  • 1 tbsp gochugaru flakes
  • 1 tbsp toasted sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar (honey, syrup, etc can be used)
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • equivalent amount chopped ginger

Directions

  1. Mix gochujang, gochugaru, sesame oil, soy sauce, and sugar in a bowl.
  2. In a saucepan over medium heat, brown the ground meat
  3. Once cooked, remove meat and drain all but 1 tbsp of oil.
  4. Cook the ginger and garlic in the remaining oil.
  5. Re-add the meat to the pan, and add the seasoning mixture.
  6. Mix well and simmer for 2-3 minutes over medium-low heat.
